UA Card Sorting
Workshop

Facilitator Guide / Internal Use Only

60 cards / single deck ~35 min per participant
02 / 09
Setup

Before you start

  • Cards printed, cut, and shuffled (different order per participant)
  • Blank sticky notes and pens on the table
  • Observation sheet ready
  • Seat next to participant, not across
  • Notepad on hand, no screen note-taking visible
03 / 09
Read Aloud

Welcome script

"Thanks for doing this. We're working on a project about how products are organised on a sporting goods website. There are no right or wrong answers: we're trying to understand how you think, not test your knowledge.

This should take about 30 to 35 minutes."

Your groups + sticky note names
"Sort the cards into groups that make sense to you. You decide how many groups. Once done, I'll ask you to name each group. Move cards anytime. If a card doesn't fit anywhere, just set it aside."
05 / 09
Read Aloud, Then Hand Over Deck

Think-aloud prompt

"One thing that helps us a lot: please think out loud as you work. Tell me what you're thinking as you group things. Even if it feels obvious, say it out loud."
If they go quiet, prompt: “What's going through your mind right now?”
Do NOT suggest groupings  |  Do NOT react with approval or disapproval
06 / 09
Use Sparingly

During-sort probes

  • “What made you put those together?”
  • “That one seemed to take a moment. What were you deciding?”
  • “You moved that card a couple of times. What changed your mind?”
  • “Is there a card that could fit in more than one group?”
07 / 09

Group naming

Say this
"Now can you write a name for each group on a sticky note and put it on top of the pile? Whatever label you'd give it on a website."
Then ask
  • “Does any group feel like it could be split into two?”
  • “Are any two groups close enough you'd consider merging them?”
08 / 09
Debrief Questions

Post-sort questions

  • 01“Was there a card that was especially hard to place? Why?”
  • 02“Were there any cards that could go in two different groups?”
  • 03“Were there any cards where the label itself was confusing?”
  • 04“Does the number of groups feel right, or would a real website have more or fewer?”
  • 05“Looking at your groups, is anything missing you'd expect to find?”
09 / 09
Session Done

Wrap up

"That's really helpful, thank you!"

Before they leave:

  • Photo the full card layout top-down
  • Write group names on observation sheet (their exact words)
  • Note any quotes or surprises

Shuffle cards, reset the table, ready for next participant.
