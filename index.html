Card Sorting — Facilitator Cheat Sheet
UA CRO Project / Sparro Internal / First-timer guide
60 cards ~35 min
1
Welcome
2 min
"Thanks for doing this. We're working on a project about how products are organised on a sporting goods website. No right or wrong answers: we want to understand how you think, not test you. About 30–35 minutes."
2
Task instructions
2 min
"Sort these cards into groups that make sense to you. You decide how many groups. Once done, give each group a name. Move cards anytime. If a card doesn't fit, set it aside."
3
Think-aloud + hand over deck
20–30 min
"Please think out loud as you work — tell me what you're thinking, even if it feels obvious."
Hand over the shuffled deck. Stay quiet. If they go silent:
"What's going through your mind right now?"
4
Group naming
2 min
"Can you write a name for each group on a sticky note and put it on top? Whatever label you'd give it on a website."
  • "Could any group split into two?"
  • "Could any two groups merge?"
?
During-sort probes
Use sparingly
  • "What made you put those together?"
  • "That took a moment. What were you deciding?"
  • "You moved that card. What changed your mind?"
  • "Could any card fit in two groups?"
5
Post-sort debrief
3–5 min
  • "Any card especially hard to place? Why?"
  • "Any cards that could go in two groups?"
  • "Any labels that were confusing?"
  • "Does the number of groups feel right?"
  • "Anything missing you'd expect to find?"
6
Wrap up + record
"That's really helpful, thank you!"
Before they leave:
  • → Photo the full layout (top-down)
  • → Write group names on observation sheet
  • → Note any quotes or surprises
Key rules
Do
  • Sit next to them
  • Note their exact words
  • Let silence sit
Do not
  • Suggest any grouping
  • Nod or react
  • Show UA navigation
